Quantitative Human Physiology

Problem Set 1 Blood

Goals: estimation, scale, google search

Many complex problems in biology can be broken down into a series of smaller problems and a common smaller problem is the estimation of some number, such as the number of bacteria per cell. This number depends on the distribution of the sizes of the bacteria, the size of the cell, and how packed the cell is with its own molecules and organelles. Here, I simply want to get you started on addressing a problem like this with very simple models of the problem. Along the way, solving the problem should give you a sense of scale of what it is like to be a bacterium or a virus living in a cellular world.

I’ll solve an analogous problem: How many beach balls can fit in a barn? Obviously the answer is dependent on the distribution of the sizes of the beach balls and the shape and size of the barn and how filled the barn is with hay (or furniture or horses or whatever). So to solve this, I need to make decisions to paramaterize the model

  1. the barn is a typical vermont barn. To find typical, I used a google search and found what looks like a reputable source that says a typical hay barn is 30 feet wide by 40 feet long. I estimated wall height from the figure as half the width and I used a 12/12 pitch for the roof, so the peak is centered and 15 feet (half of the width) high.

  2. I used a big beach ball of 2 feet in diameter (because big beach balls are fun).

  3. 10% of the barn is filled with hay.

1.1 Problem set

Do these on the same sheet. Name the sheet “1. Blood”

  1. How many red blood cells in a drop of blood?

  2. How many bacteria could colonize an empty red blood cell?

  3. How many bacteria could colonize an empty macrophage?

  4. How many virus particles could colonize an empty red blood cell?

These should all be on the same google sheet. Do not hardcode parameters, that is, if a virus is 30 feet wide do not put “30” in an equation but instead make your equations reference the cell with this information. You will need to google search information to parameterize the model, such as, how big a virus is. Part of the goal of this is for you to develop your skills finding reliable information using a google search. There is variation in virus size or cell size so use something in the middle or “typical”. Cite a webpage giving the source as above.
