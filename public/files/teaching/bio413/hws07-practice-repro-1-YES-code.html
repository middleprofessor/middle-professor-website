Part I Gut microorganisms act together to exacerbate inflammation in spinal cords

The assignment is: reproduce the analysis of the experiment presented in Fig 1G of the article

Miyauchi, Eiji, et al. “Gut microorganisms act together to exacerbate inflammation in spinal cords.” Nature 585.7823 (2020): 102-106.

dataset: Miyauchi-2020-fig1f,g-gut-microorganisms.xlsx

You need to 1) import the data, 2) analyze the data, and 3) report the results following the example in Section 10.1.1

About the data: The data file contains the percent of cells expressing the genes ki67+ and CD4+ in Fig 1F and the total number of CD4+ T cells in Fig 1G. ki67 is expressed prior to cell division (its a “marker” for cell division). CD4 is expressed on Helper T cells (its a “marker” for helper T cells) – a type of lymphocyte that functions in the adaptive immune response.

The researchers are interested in the role of intestinal bacteria on the development of autoimmune diseases and have a mouse model for the autoimmune disease multiple sclerosis by inducing experimental autoimmune encephalomyelitis (EAE). The goal of the paper is to explore connections between intestinal bacteria, the immune response to these bacteria, and the development of multiple sclerosis like phenotypes. In the experiments in Fig 1, the researchers are measuring the effect of induced EAE on different immune responses. Fig. 1F, G is looking at the response on CD4+ T cells and especially CD4+ T cells that also express ki67.

Response variable:

  1. Fig 1F: Percentage of CD4+ T cells.
  2. Fig 1G: Number of CD4+ T cells. ONLY analyze Fig 1G

Note. Percentage and count data can never be normally distributed but they can sometimes look like data that were sampled from a normal distribution. This is a good example of the debate in the Ecology literature of using more advanced methods (generalized linear models) that we’ll learn in a few weeks or using the simpler methods (general linear models) that we’re using now.

The treatment factor in the experiment reported in Fig. 1F, G has three levels

  1. Naive – Mice that have not been induced to develop EAE. The researchers expect the percentage/counts to be low.
  2. Control – Mice that have been induced to develop EAE (the model for multiple sclerosis). The researchers expect the percentage/counts to be high.
  3. Amp – EAE mice given the antibiotic ampicillin, which kills the bacteria that induce EAE. The researchers expect to see percentage/counts lower than the control, given results earlier in figure.

# effect of dll4 with and without ovx

# (dll4 - control) = effect of dll4 without ovx
planned <- list(
  "dll4 - control" = dll4 - control,
  "ovx - control" = ovx - control,
  "interaction" = (both - ovx) - (dll4 - control)
)
